Problem Statement

Urban residents want to recycle more effectively, but inconsistent local recycling rules and lack of clear information make it confusing to know what items can be recycled.

Lack of Information

Residents often don’t know which plastics are recyclable.

Instructions vary by city and aren’t easily accessible.

Recycling bins rarely include clear visual guides.

Inconsistent Policies

Different municipalities have different recycling rules.

Recycling companies use different symbols and standards.

National recycling guidelines are unclear or outdated.

Behavioral Challenges

People throw recyclables in trash when unsure.

Apartment dwellers lack convenient recycling options.

Recycling feels inconvenient or time-consuming.

Environmental Impact

Contaminated recycling leads to landfill overflow.

Improper sorting increases processing costs.

Low recycling rates contribute to higher carbon emissions.
